# Lightweight Reporting Dashboard Automation

## Offer Summary

A narrow automation package for small businesses that need a simple, repeatable view of important numbers without manually copying data between spreadsheets, inboxes, CRMs, forms, calendars, or payment tools. The system collects defined source data, refreshes a lightweight dashboard, and highlights the few metrics or exceptions that need owner attention.

## Target Client

Small service businesses, agencies, local operators, consultants, and lean teams that already track activity in simple tools but do not have a reliable weekly or monthly reporting workflow.

Good examples include businesses tracking leads, appointments, estimates, invoices, project status, support requests, delivery milestones, client onboarding, or recurring admin work.

## Business Problem

Owners often know the business has useful data, but it is scattered across tools and updated inconsistently. Manual reporting wastes time, creates stale numbers, and makes it harder to spot follow-up gaps, bottlenecks, overdue work, and revenue opportunities.

## Automation Solution

Create a lightweight reporting workflow that gathers agreed source data, normalizes the key fields, refreshes a dashboard or tracker, and sends a recurring summary with metrics, exceptions, and recommended follow-up items.

## Included Scope

- Review of current reporting sources and decision needs.
- Definition of 5 to 10 practical metrics or status fields.
- Data collection from one or two simple sources, such as a spreadsheet, form, CRM export, inbox label, calendar, or task board.
- Dashboard or tracker setup in a spreadsheet, lightweight database, or existing business tool.
- Recurring summary email, task, or notification for the owner or team.
- Exception flags for items such as stale leads, overdue tasks, missing client details, failed handoffs, unpaid invoices, or upcoming deadlines.
- Plain-language operating notes, refresh rules, and test cases.

## Best Fit

This offer is a strong fit when the client:

- already tracks important work but does not review it consistently
- wants visibility without adopting a heavy BI platform
- can identify the few numbers or statuses that actually drive decisions
- has one or two reasonably clean data sources to start from
- needs recurring summaries, exception alerts, or a simple owner dashboard

## Not A Fit

This is not the right first offer when the client needs enterprise analytics, complex data warehousing, deeply custom forecasting, regulated financial reporting, or major cleanup across many inconsistent systems before any useful dashboard can exist.

## Expected Outcome

The client gets a repeatable reporting view that saves manual update time, makes important gaps easier to see, and creates a simple habit around reviewing the business metrics that matter most.

## Discovery Questions

1. What decisions should this dashboard help you make each week or month?
2. Which tools currently hold the source data?
3. What numbers or statuses do you currently check manually?
4. Which gaps or exceptions are most expensive to miss?
5. How often should the dashboard refresh or summary be sent?
6. Who needs to see the dashboard or recurring summary?
7. What fields are reliable today, and which ones are often missing or inconsistent?
8. Are any data sources sensitive, regulated, or restricted from automation?

## Inputs Needed From Client

- Current reporting process and sample reports, if any.
- Access to or exports from the initial data source systems.
- Metric definitions and what each status should mean.
- Preferred dashboard location, such as spreadsheet, CRM view, database, or reporting tool.
- Summary timing, recipients, and escalation rules.
- Any privacy, compliance, or access-control requirements.

## Delivery Phases

### Phase 1: Define Reporting Decisions

Clarify the decisions, metrics, source systems, refresh rhythm, and exception rules that make the dashboard useful without overbuilding it.

### Phase 2: Map And Clean Source Data

Identify required fields, normalize naming and statuses, and document any missing or unreliable data that needs manual fallback.

### Phase 3: Build Dashboard And Summary Workflow

Create the dashboard or tracker, connect the initial source data, add exception flags, and configure the recurring summary or notification.

### Phase 4: Test And Handoff

Run sample records through the workflow, verify dashboard accuracy, confirm summary timing, and hand off operating notes for ongoing use.

## Risks And Constraints

- Dashboard quality depends on reliable source data and clear metric definitions.
- Some systems may require exports or API access rather than direct automation.
- Sensitive, financial, health, or regulated data may require human compliance review before automation.
- The starter package should stay focused on one dashboard, one reporting rhythm, and one or two data sources.

## Suggested Starting Price Logic

Price based on number of data sources, metric complexity, refresh frequency, exception logic, and whether the summary requires approvals or role-based visibility. Keep the starter package focused on a simple owner dashboard, then expand once the client trusts the core reporting loop.

## CTA / Next Step

Offer a reporting workflow audit: review where the client's key numbers live today, identify the highest-value weekly summary, and map a lightweight dashboard that turns scattered updates into a simple operating view.
