# Appointment Reminder Automation

## Offer Summary

A narrow automation package for appointment-based service businesses that need fewer missed bookings, clearer client communication, and less manual reminder work. The system sends timely reminders, tracks confirmation status, flags at-risk appointments, and gives the business owner a simple view of what needs attention.

## Target Client

Local and professional service businesses that book appointments, consultations, estimates, sessions, calls, or visits through email, forms, calendars, phone calls, or a lightweight CRM.

Good examples include consultants, home service providers, clinics with non-sensitive admin workflows, coaches, agencies, repair businesses, and appointment-based local operators.

## Business Problem

Missed appointments and late cancellations waste time and revenue. Many small teams still rely on manual texts, emails, calendar checks, or memory to confirm bookings. When reminders are inconsistent, clients forget, staff scramble, and open time slots cannot be recovered quickly.

## Automation Solution

Create a reminder workflow that triggers from the client's booking source, sends one or more confirmation/reminder messages, records responses or status, and alerts the business when an appointment is unconfirmed or needs manual follow-up.

## Included Scope

- Appointment source review and trigger setup.
- Reminder message sequence, such as confirmation, 24-hour reminder, and same-day reminder.
- Confirmation or status tracking in a spreadsheet, CRM, task board, or calendar-friendly tracker.
- Owner alert for unconfirmed, missing-detail, cancelled, or reschedule-needed appointments.
- Optional draft-first messaging when the business wants human review before reminders go out.
- Basic no-show and cancellation summary fields for later review.
- Plain-language operating notes and test cases for ongoing use.

## Best Fit

This offer is a strong fit when the client:

- books recurring appointments or consultations
- loses time to no-shows, late cancellations, or repeated manual reminders
- already has a calendar, booking form, CRM, spreadsheet, or inbox-based booking process
- wants a practical automation that supports staff rather than replacing judgment
- can define when reminders should be sent and what clients should receive

## Not A Fit

This is not the right first offer when the client has no consistent booking source, needs complex medical/legal compliance review before any messaging, or wants a full scheduling platform replacement instead of a reminder workflow.

## Expected Outcome

The client gets more consistent reminder coverage, clearer appointment status, fewer manual follow-up tasks, and faster visibility into which bookings need attention before the appointment time.

## Discovery Questions

1. How are appointments currently booked?
2. Where is the source of truth for appointment date, time, client name, and contact details?
3. What reminders are sent today, and by whom?
4. When should reminders go out?
5. What should count as confirmed, cancelled, or needing follow-up?
6. What communication channels are acceptable for reminders?
7. Who should be alerted when a booking is unconfirmed or missing details?
8. Are there any compliance, consent, or industry-specific messaging rules that require human review?

## Inputs Needed From Client

- Current booking workflow and sample appointment records.
- Reminder timing preferences.
- Approved message language for confirmation, reminder, cancellation, and reschedule prompts.
- Tool access or exports for calendar, CRM, form, spreadsheet, or email systems involved.
- Rules for human review, opt-outs, sensitive data, and escalation.

## Delivery Phases

### Phase 1: Map Booking And Reminder Flow

Document booking sources, required fields, reminder timing, confirmation rules, and manual follow-up points.

### Phase 2: Design The Reminder Sequence

Define triggers, message templates, status fields, escalation rules, and any human review checkpoints.

### Phase 3: Build And Connect

Configure the reminder workflow, tracking view, alerts, and sample records using the client's existing systems where possible.

### Phase 4: Test And Handoff

Run test appointments through each reminder path, verify opt-out or manual review behavior, and hand off operating notes.

## Risks And Constraints

- Reminder reliability depends on clean appointment data and stable source systems.
- SMS, email, or platform messaging may require consent, sender setup, or usage limits.
- Regulated industries need human review of compliance and privacy requirements before automation goes live.
- The workflow should include clear manual fallback steps when contact details are missing or a message fails.

## Suggested Starting Price Logic

Price based on number of booking sources, reminder channels, confirmation logic, and reporting needs. Keep the starter package focused on one booking source and one primary reminder channel, then expand after the base workflow proves reliable.

## CTA / Next Step

Offer a no-show reduction workflow audit: review the client's current booking and reminder process, identify where reminders are missed, and map a simple automation that confirms appointments and flags follow-up before time is lost.
