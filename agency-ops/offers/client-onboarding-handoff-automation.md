# Client Onboarding Handoff Automation

## Offer Summary

A focused automation package for service businesses that lose momentum after a prospect says yes. The system turns a won lead into a clean onboarding flow with intake, internal handoff notes, task creation, reminders, and client-facing next steps.

## Target Client

Small service businesses, consultants, agencies, and local operators that sell through calls, email, forms, or referrals and then manually coordinate onboarding across inboxes, spreadsheets, calendars, and documents.

## Business Problem

Many teams are reasonably good at closing work but inconsistent at starting it. After a client agrees to move forward, details are scattered across email, notes, calendars, payment status, intake forms, and internal memory. That creates slow starts, missed setup steps, repeated questions, and a weaker first client experience.

## Automation Solution

Build a lightweight onboarding handoff workflow that captures the required client details, routes them to the right internal place, creates the first delivery tasks, sends clear next-step communication, and reminds the owner when something is missing.

## Included Scope

- Onboarding intake form or structured intake email template.
- Internal handoff summary generated from client-provided details.
- Task or checklist creation in the client's preferred workspace, such as a spreadsheet, project board, CRM, or task manager.
- Client next-step email draft or automated confirmation message.
- Missing-information reminders for the business owner or client.
- Simple delivery-start tracker showing onboarding status, owner, next action, and blockers.
- Plain-language operating notes for how to run and maintain the workflow.

## Best Fit

This offer is a strong fit when the client:

- already has leads or clients coming in
- closes work through calls, email, referrals, or forms
- repeats the same onboarding questions for most clients
- manually copies details into multiple tools
- has missed setup steps, slow starts, or unclear internal handoffs
- wants a practical workflow rather than a full custom software build

## Not A Fit

This is not the right first offer when the client has no repeatable onboarding process, needs a full CRM rebuild, requires complex compliance review, or has not decided what information must be collected before delivery begins.

## Expected Outcome

The client gets a more reliable handoff from sale to delivery, fewer dropped details, faster onboarding starts, and clearer visibility into what is waiting on the client versus what the business needs to do next.

## Discovery Questions

1. What happens today immediately after a prospect says yes?
2. What information must be collected before delivery can start?
3. Where do client details currently live after the sale?
4. What setup steps are repeated for every new client?
5. Which steps are most often delayed, forgotten, or unclear?
6. Who needs to be notified internally when a new client starts?
7. What tools should the workflow use, such as email, forms, spreadsheets, CRM, calendar, or task manager?
8. What message should the client receive after they commit?

## Inputs Needed From Client

- Current onboarding steps and any existing checklist.
- Sample intake questions, forms, emails, or handoff notes.
- Tool access or example exports for the systems involved.
- Preferred owner for each onboarding step.
- Client-facing tone and brand preferences for confirmation or next-step emails.

## Delivery Phases

### Phase 1: Map The Current Handoff

Document the existing post-sale flow, required client inputs, internal owner responsibilities, and recurring failure points.

### Phase 2: Design The Workflow

Define the trigger, intake structure, routing rules, reminders, status fields, and client-facing communication.

### Phase 3: Build The Automation

Configure the form, email, tracker, tasks, reminders, and handoff summary using the smallest reliable toolchain that fits the client's current systems.

### Phase 4: Test And Handoff

Run sample onboarding cases, verify failure paths, document the operating process, and hand off a concise checklist for ongoing use.

## Risks And Constraints

- Automation quality depends on the client having a clear minimum onboarding checklist.
- Tool access and permissions may limit what can be automated directly.
- Client-specific compliance, payment, or contract requirements need human review before automation.
- The system should include a clear human review point before any legally or financially sensitive communication.

## Suggested Starting Price Logic

Price based on number of systems involved, complexity of routing/reminders, and whether client-facing messaging is automated or draft-first. Keep the initial package narrow and expand only after the base onboarding handoff works reliably.

## CTA / Next Step

Offer a short onboarding audit: review the client's current post-sale handoff, identify the top three dropped-detail risks, and propose a simple automation map for turning a won lead into a delivery-ready client record.
