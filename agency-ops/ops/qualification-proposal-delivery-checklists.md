# Qualification, Proposal, And Delivery Checklists

## Lead Qualification Checklist

Use this when a new lead, prospect, or opportunity appears.

- Identify the business type and decision maker.
- Capture the current pain point in plain language.
- Estimate pain severity: low, medium, or high.
- Estimate automation value: low, medium, or high.
- Confirm the systems involved, such as email, forms, spreadsheets, CRM, scheduling, or billing.
- Identify urgency and timeline.
- Note likely budget sensitivity.
- Decide stage: poor fit, nurture, discovery needed, qualified, or proposal ready.
- Record the next action and owner.

## Proposal Readiness Checklist

Use this before drafting a proposal.

- Business problem is specific and worth solving.
- Desired outcome is clear enough to describe without hype.
- Scope can be bounded into phases or deliverables.
- Required client inputs are known.
- Major dependencies and risks are visible.
- Success criteria are practical and observable.
- Out-of-scope items are listed.
- Human approvals, credentials, and access needs are identified.
- Recommended next step is either discovery, proposal review, or delivery kickoff.

## Delivery Planning Checklist

Use this after work is won or ready for implementation planning.

- Define the workflow trigger, inputs, outputs, and owner.
- List all connected systems and permissions needed.
- Map the happy path and the common failure paths.
- Define testing data or sample cases.
- Identify manual review points and escalation steps.
- Create a handoff note for operating the automation.
- Record maintenance expectations and future improvement options.
- Save delivery materials under `agency-ops/delivery/` or a client-specific folder.

## Closeout Checklist

Use this when a workflow is delivered.

- Confirm the workflow was tested with realistic examples.
- Document how to run, monitor, pause, or update it.
- Record any known limitations.
- Capture follow-up or expansion opportunities.
- Move the pipeline stage to delivery complete or follow-up/expansion.
