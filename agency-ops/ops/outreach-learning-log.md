## What To Log

Log a learning entry when any of these happen:

- Draft copy is reviewed or revised.
- A message is sent.
- A prospect replies.
- A prospect does not reply after the follow-up window.
- A follow-up is drafted or sent.
- A discovery call happens.
- A pricing, CRM, timing, compliance, or no-interest objection appears.
- A page, offer, FAQ, or proposal asset needs to change based on market signal.

## Objection Taxonomy

Use these categories when logging replies or discovery comments.

| Objection | Meaning | Best response path | Asset to improve |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Already has CRM | Prospect may have tools but still may have workflow gaps | Offer workflow map around current system | Scope menu, reply kit, discovery script |
| Not looking for automation | Low urgency or unclear pain | Offer diagnostic map or close politely | Outreach copy, target filters |
| Price first | Prospect wants cost before scope | Route to scope menu; do not quote final price | Scope menu, proposal-readiness snapshot |
| Too busy | Timing friction | Offer small async workflow map or nurture | Follow-up template, discovery script |
| Who are you? | Trust gap | Clarify narrow offer and low-risk first step | Website, about page, proof language |
| Does this auto-text/email leads? | Safety and compliance concern | Start with internal reminders/drafts | Reply kit, FAQ, proposal safety language |
| We use phone calls mostly | Intake source mismatch | Ask whether phone and form leads are tracked together | Discovery script, offer framing |
| No need / not interested | Poor fit or timing | Close or nurture, no pressure | Lead filters, targeting |

## Current Baseline Hypotheses

These are assumptions to validate, not proven facts.

1. Home-services companies with visible estimate or quote forms may value clearer routing and follow-up visibility.
2. Specific references to public intake fields should outperform generic automation language.
3. Workflow-map language should feel safer than CRM replacement or AI transformation language.
4. Internal reminders and owner visibility should be a safer first sell than auto-send behavior.
5. A three-lead first batch is small enough to learn without creating follow-up chaos.

## Asset Update Rules

Update assets only from meaningful repeated signal or strong single-prospect evidence.

- Update outreach copy when subject lines, opening hooks, or CTAs create confusion or objections.
- Update FAQ when prospects ask the same question more than once.
- Update scope menu when package boundaries are unclear.
- Update proposal assembly kit when proposal facts repeat across discovery calls.
- Update website brief or copy when public positioning creates trust or clarity issues.
- Update delivery plan starter when real delivery assumptions become common.
