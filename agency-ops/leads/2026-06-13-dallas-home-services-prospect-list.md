# Dallas Home Services Prospect List

## Purpose

Create the first real prospecting list for Kelvar's lead intake and follow-up automation offer. This list focuses on Dallas/Fort Worth home-service businesses with visible estimate, quote, contact, or booking paths where a missed lead or slow follow-up can have real revenue cost.

## Target Niche

Dallas/Fort Worth home-service operators with quote-based or appointment-based intake.

Best initial offer angle: lead intake command system or follow-up engine.

## Qualification Method

Sources reviewed were public website/search results as of 2026-06-13. A prospect is included when public information shows at least one clear intake path such as estimate request, quote request, contact form, phone booking, free consultation, or online scheduling.

Do not create individual lead records or send outreach until the user explicitly chooses prospects to pursue.

## Outreach Angle By Segment

### HVAC

Observed pattern: estimate/service requests come through forms and phone CTAs, often with scheduling expectations.

Angle: help route estimate requests, flag missing details, remind the admin/owner when a lead has not been contacted, and create a simple status view for new, contacted, scheduled, quoted, and closed requests.

### Roofing

Observed pattern: quote, inspection, commercial, residential, and insurance-related requests may need different next steps.

Angle: help classify estimate requests by job type, assign the right follow-up path, and keep inspection/quote status visible without forcing a full CRM rebuild.

### Landscaping

Observed pattern: quote forms often collect property type, service interest, and location details.

Angle: help route quote requests by service type or property type, prevent slow follow-up, and create a simple owner/admin view of open quote opportunities.
