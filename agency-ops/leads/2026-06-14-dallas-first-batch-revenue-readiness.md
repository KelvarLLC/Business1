## Scoring Method

Score each prospect from 1 to 5 across five practical sales-readiness factors.

- Intake signal clarity: public evidence that estimate or quote intake exists.
- Pain plausibility: likelihood that routing, follow-up, or visibility matters.
- Offer fit: match to Lead Intake Command System / Follow-Up Engine.
- Outreach specificity: ability to write a relevant message without guessing.
- Risk control: ability to avoid unsupported claims, regulated promises, or overreach.

Total possible score: 25.

Decision bands:

- 21-25: strong first-batch prospect
- 17-20: usable with careful positioning
- 13-16: nurture or research more
- 0-12: hold

## Prospect-Specific Readiness Notes

### DFW HVAC

Why it is strong:

- Public estimate page creates a clear workflow surface.
- The 2-business-hour response expectation makes follow-up timing easy to discuss.
- The offer can be framed around protecting their existing response rhythm, not replacing their process.

Best message angle:

> I noticed your estimate page sets a clear response expectation. I help service businesses keep those requests organized and visible so the next follow-up does not depend on manual tracking alone.

Avoid saying:

- They miss leads.
- Their current system is broken.
- Automation will guarantee faster sales.

## Objection Handling Before Outreach

### Objection: We already have a CRM.

Response direction:

- Respect the existing system.
- Offer to map the handoff around the current CRM rather than replace it.
- Focus on missed handoffs, visibility, and follow-up timing.

Useful line:

> That is exactly why I would start with a workflow map, not a rebuild. The question is whether the current intake path leaves any gaps between form submission, owner review, scheduling, and follow-up.

### Objection: We are not looking for automation.

Response direction:

- Keep it light.
- Offer the map as a diagnostic, not a commitment.
- Do not argue.

Useful line:

> Totally fair. The smallest useful version is just a quick map of where requests enter, who sees them, and where follow-up can stall.

### Objection: How much does it cost?

Response direction:

- Do not quote final pricing from email alone.
- Route to the scope menu.
- Offer to identify the smallest package path first.

Useful line:

> It depends on whether the best first step is a workflow blueprint, a single-source intake build, or a follow-up visibility upgrade. I would want to see the current intake path before putting a real number on it.

### Objection: Can it automatically text/email leads?

Response direction:

- Keep first phase focused on internal routing and reminders.
- Auto-send requires explicit approval and review.
- Avoid compliance advice.

Useful line:

> It can be designed that way later, but I would start with internal routing and follow-up reminders first. Auto-send behavior should be reviewed separately so the business stays in control.
