## Scoring Method

Score each prospect from 1 to 5 across five practical sales-readiness factors.

- Intake signal clarity: public evidence that estimate, quote, or contact intake exists.
- Pain plausibility: likelihood that routing, follow-up, or visibility matters.
- Offer fit: match to Lead Intake Command System / Follow-Up Engine.
- Outreach specificity: ability to write a relevant message without guessing.
- Risk control: ability to avoid unsupported claims, regulated promises, or overreach.

Total possible score: 25.

Decision bands:

- 21-25: strong outreach prospect
- 17-20: usable with careful positioning
- 13-16: nurture or research more
- 0-12: hold

## Objection Handling Before Outreach

### Objection: We already have a form or CRM.

Response direction:

- Affirm the existing system.
- Position the offer as a workflow map around the current setup.
- Focus on handoff visibility, not replacement.

Useful line:

> That is a good starting point. I would not suggest replacing it first; I would map what happens after the request arrives and look for the smallest place follow-up can become clearer.

### Objection: We do not need automation right now.

Response direction:

- Keep the reply low-pressure.
- Offer the map as a diagnostic.
- Close gracefully if there is no pain.

Useful line:

> Totally fair. The smallest useful version is just a quick look at where requests enter, who owns the next step, and whether anything is being tracked manually.

### Objection: How much does it cost?

Response direction:

- Do not quote final pricing from email alone.
- Route to the Lead Intake scope menu.
- Identify the smallest package path first.

Useful line:

> It depends on whether the right first step is a workflow blueprint, a single-source intake build, or a follow-up visibility upgrade. I would want to see the current intake path before putting a real number on it.

### Objection: Can this text or email prospects automatically?

Response direction:

- Start with internal routing and reminders.
- Keep auto-send as a separate approval-gated later phase.
- Avoid compliance advice.

Useful line:

> It can be designed that way later, but I would start with internal routing and follow-up reminders first. Auto-send behavior should be reviewed separately so the business stays in control.
