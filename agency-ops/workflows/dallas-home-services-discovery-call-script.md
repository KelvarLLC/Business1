# Dallas Home Services Discovery Call Script

## Purpose

Give Kelvar LLC a practical discovery structure for any positive reply from the Dallas/Fort Worth home-services lead batch.

This script supports the Lead Intake Command System / Follow-Up Engine offer across:

- HVAC estimate requests
- Roofing estimate, inspection, repair, and storm-damage inquiries
- Landscaping quote requests
- Commercial property service requests

This script does not approve outreach, sending, pricing, or implementation. Use it only after a prospect replies positively or asks for a call/review.

## Call Goal

Decide whether the prospect has a real intake or follow-up pain worth moving to:

- workflow blueprint
- single-source intake build
- follow-up visibility upgrade
- nurture
- close/no-fit

The call should identify the smallest useful first win, not sell a broad AI transformation.

## Opening

Use a simple, direct opener:

> Thanks for taking the time. The goal today is not to overhaul your systems. I want to understand what happens after a quote, estimate, or contact request comes in, where follow-up can get fuzzy, and whether there is a small workflow that would make the next step clearer for your team.

Set expectations:

- 15 to 25 minutes is enough for an initial map.
- No sensitive customer data is needed.
- The output is a recommended next step, not a forced implementation.

## Core Discovery Questions

### 1. Intake Sources

Ask:

- Where do new quote, estimate, inspection, or contact requests come from today?
- Which sources matter most: website form, phone, email, ads, referrals, chat, social, or marketplace leads?
- Do all requests land in one place, or are they split across inboxes, forms, phones, and notes?

Listen for:

- Multiple intake sources.
- Manual copying or forwarding.
- Requests that depend on one person noticing them.
- No single view of open requests.

### 2. First Response Ownership

Ask:

- Who sees a new request first?
- Who decides whether it is urgent, qualified, or ready for scheduling?
- What happens when the first owner is busy or out?
- Is there a response-time expectation your team tries to hit?

Listen for:

- Undefined owner.
- Multiple possible owners.
- Response-time commitments.
- Risk around missed calls, after-hours forms, or busy seasons.

### 3. Routing And Classification

Ask:

- What details change how a request should be handled?
- Do different service types route to different people?
- Do budget, property type, location, urgency, or job type change the next step?
- Are there requests that should be fast-tracked or filtered out?

Listen for:

- Service categories that need different handling.
- Commercial versus residential routing.
- Emergency or urgent categories.
- Project size or budget qualification.

### 4. Follow-Up And Status

Ask:

- After the first response, how do you know what still needs follow-up?
- Where do statuses like new, contacted, scheduled, quoted, won, lost, or waiting live?
- What happens if the customer does not answer?
- Do reminders happen automatically, manually, or not at all?

Listen for:

- No clear follow-up owner.
- Manual reminders.
- Spreadsheet or inbox-only tracking.
- Quotes or estimates with no next-step visibility.

### 5. Tools And Constraints

Ask:

- What tools are involved today: CRM, field-service software, email, calendar, spreadsheet, form builder, or dispatch platform?
- Which tool is non-negotiable and should not be replaced?
- Are there automations already running?
- Are there any compliance, consent, or customer-communication rules the team follows?

Listen for:

- Existing CRM that should be respected.
- Manual work between tools.
- Sensitivity around texting or auto-email.
- Need to start with internal reminders before external automation.

## Vertical-Specific Question Modules

Use only the relevant module.

### HVAC

Ask:

- Are repair, replacement, maintenance, and commercial service requests handled differently?
- Do emergency or no-cool/no-heat situations get routed differently from estimate requests?
- Do form requests and phone calls end up in the same tracking path?
- Is there a response-time target, such as 2 business hours or same day?

Likely first wins:

- Service-type classification.
- Follow-up timer for estimate requests.
- Internal reminder before response window is missed.
- Status tracker for new, contacted, scheduled, quoted, and closed.

### Roofing

Ask:

- Are residential, commercial, repair, inspection, and storm-damage requests routed differently?
- Are insurance-claim-related details captured during intake?
- Are photos, project descriptions, and preferred appointment times reviewed before scheduling?
- Are phone and form inquiries tracked together?

Likely first wins:

- Request classification by roofing category.
- Photo/project detail flagging.
- Inspection or estimate follow-up tracker.
- Internal reminders before open inquiries sit too long.

### Landscaping And Property Services

Ask:

- Are residential, commercial, tree-care, maintenance, and one-time project requests routed differently?
- Do property type, budget range, service interest, and location change who follows up?
- What happens when a preferred contact method or best call time is captured?
- Is quote status visible after the first response?

Likely first wins:

- Quote-routing board by property type and service interest.
- Best-time-to-call reminder.
- Budget or project-size prioritization view.
- Open quote status tracker.

## Pain Severity Scoring During Call

Score quietly from 1 to 5.

| Factor | 1 | 3 | 5 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Intake volume | Rare requests | Steady but manageable | Frequent or high-value requests |
| Manual handling | Mostly automated | Some manual forwarding/tracking | Heavy inbox, phone, notes, or spreadsheet work |
| Follow-up risk | Clear owner and reminders | Some ambiguity | Requests can sit or get lost |
| Tool fit | No accessible workflow surface | One workable source | Clear form/inbox/CRM handoff to improve |
| Buyer urgency | Curious only | Sees some friction | Wants improvement soon |

Interpretation:

- 20-25: strong proposal candidate
- 15-19: discovery or blueprint candidate
- 10-14: nurture or lightweight recommendation
- Below 10: hold or close

## Scope Path Decision

Use the Lead Intake scope menu.

### Workflow Blueprint

Best when:

- The prospect is interested but not ready to build.
- Current tools are unclear.
- Several intake paths need mapping first.

Deliverable:

- Current-state workflow map.
- Pain points and handoffs.
- Smallest recommended automation path.

### Single-Source Intake Build

Best when:

- One form, inbox, or request source is the obvious starting point.
- The team needs routing, owner assignment, and follow-up visibility.
- The first win can be delivered without a full CRM rebuild.

Deliverable:

- Intake capture and classification.
- Owner/status tracking.
- Internal follow-up reminders.
- Simple handoff guide.

### Follow-Up Visibility Upgrade

Best when:

- The prospect already receives leads reliably.
- The pain is tracking open follow-up, scheduled calls, quotes, or unanswered prospects.
- Existing tools should remain in place.

Deliverable:

- Status view.
- Follow-up due dates.
- Internal reminders.
- Weekly owner summary if useful.

## Red Flags

Pause or hold if:

- They want guaranteed revenue claims.
- They want legal, insurance, or compliance advice.
- They want auto-send behavior before internal workflow control is understood.
- They cannot identify an owner or tool for the workflow.
- The request requires access to sensitive production systems before scope is defined.
- The request is outside `KelvarLLC/Business1` business operations or cannot be documented safely.

## Closing The Call

Use one of these closes.

### If Strong Fit

> Based on what you described, the strongest first step is not a huge rebuild. It is a focused workflow around `[intake source]` so new requests are classified, owned, and followed up without relying on manual tracking. I can turn this into a short scope outline for review.

Next internal action:

- Update lead record.
- Score with proposal decision scorecard.
- Draft proposal outline only if score supports it.

### If Discovery Needed

> I think there may be a useful workflow here, but I would want to map the intake path before recommending a build. The right first step is a simple blueprint of where requests come in, who sees them, and where follow-up can stall.

Next internal action:

- Update lead record.
- Recommend workflow blueprint.
- Capture missing inputs.

### If Not Fit Yet

> It sounds like the current process is either working well enough or the timing is not right. I can leave you with the simple checklist I use: where requests enter, who owns the first response, how status is tracked, and when follow-up is due.

Next internal action:

- Move to nurture or closed-lost.
- Record reason.

## Post-Call Repository Update Checklist

After any discovery call, update or create:

- Lead record under `agency-ops/leads/`
- Follow-up tracker row
- Discovery notes if useful
- Proposal decision scorecard if the lead is close to proposal-ready
- Proposal outline only if scorecard supports it

Do not update public proof, pricing, website launch status, deployment status, or client claims from a discovery call alone.
