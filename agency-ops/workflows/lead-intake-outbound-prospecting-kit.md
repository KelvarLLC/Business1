# Lead Intake Outbound Prospecting Kit

## Purpose

Create a repeatable way to find, qualify, and approach small service businesses that are likely losing opportunities because lead intake and follow-up are scattered across forms, email, phone notes, spreadsheets, social messages, or scheduling tools.

This workflow supports the `lead-intake-follow-up-automation` offer and should be used before creating individual lead records under `agency-ops/leads/`.

## Primary Offer

Lead intake and follow-up automation: a focused workflow that captures inquiries, structures key details, assigns a stage, and prompts timely follow-up without forcing a full CRM rebuild.

## Best Initial Niches

Prioritize appointment-based or quote-based service businesses where missed follow-up has an obvious revenue cost.

Strong first-pass niches:

- Home service contractors that request estimates through website forms.
- Specialty repair companies with phone, email, and form-based inquiries.
- Med spas, wellness clinics, and coaching practices with consultation requests.
- Consultants and professional service firms that receive discovery-call inquiries.
- Local agencies or studios that manage leads through email and spreadsheets.
- Training, tutoring, or lesson-based businesses that handle inquiries manually.

Avoid as first-pass targets:

- Businesses with no visible inquiry path.
- Highly regulated workflows where messaging or data handling needs legal review before discovery.
- Enterprise teams that already have mature CRM operations.
- Companies asking for broad revenue guarantees instead of process improvement.

## Prospect Qualification Filters

A prospect is worth adding to a lead record when at least three of these signals are visible or confirmed:

- The website has a contact form, estimate form, booking request, or consultation CTA.
- The business sells a service where one missed lead could be worth meaningful revenue.
- There are multiple likely intake channels, such as website, phone, email, social, and scheduling links.
- The business appears owner-led or admin-light, making manual follow-up likely.
- Reviews, website copy, or service pages show recurring appointment, estimate, or consultation demand.
- The current intake path does not clearly explain what happens after submission.
- Follow-up speed, quote turnaround, or scheduling clarity would be an obvious operational advantage.

Disqualify or defer when:

- The business does not appear to accept inbound leads.
- The site is inactive or the business status is unclear.
- The workflow appears mostly emergency-only and phone-first with little automation surface.
- The likely data involved is sensitive enough to require compliance review before any proposal.

Use this table format when building a real prospect list. Do not fabricate company details; mark unknowns clearly.

## Research Workflow

1. Pick one niche and one geography or service category.
2. Identify 10 to 20 candidate businesses with visible inquiry or booking paths.
3. Review each website for intake signals, contact paths, and follow-up friction.
4. Score each candidate as poor fit, nurture, or ready for discovery.
5. Create lead records only for candidates with enough evidence to justify follow-up.
6. Draft outreach from the observed business pain, not from a generic automation pitch.

## Discovery Trigger

Move a prospect to `discovery-needed` when there is evidence that:

- inbound inquiries are important to revenue,
- follow-up or qualification is likely manual,
- the business has a clear decision maker or contact path,
- a narrow automation audit would be easy to explain.

## Outreach Angle

Use a practical, low-pressure angle:

"I noticed your business has a few ways for new inquiries to come in. I help service businesses tighten up lead intake and follow-up so form submissions, emails, and calls do not depend on someone manually remembering every next step. Would it be useful if I mapped the simplest version of that workflow for your current process?"

## First Email Draft

Subject: Quick question about lead follow-up

Hi `[Name]`,

I was looking at how new inquiries come into `[Business]` and noticed there may be a few different paths for people to ask about service, quotes, or appointments.

I build lightweight automation workflows for service businesses that capture new leads, organize the key details, and remind the owner or admin team what needs follow-up next. The goal is not a full CRM rebuild; it is fewer missed inquiries and a cleaner response rhythm.

Would it be useful if I mapped a simple lead intake and follow-up workflow around your current process?

Best,
Jameson
Kelvar LLC

## Follow-Up Draft

Subject: Re: lead follow-up

Hi `[Name]`,

Just following up on this. The quick win I usually look for is whether new inquiries are being copied between forms, email, spreadsheets, calendars, or notes by hand.

If that is happening, a small workflow can usually make the next step clearer without changing the whole way the business operates.

Worth a quick look?

Best,
Jameson

## Call / Discovery Questions

1. Where do new inquiries come from today?
2. Who sees the inquiry first?
3. What counts as a qualified lead?
4. How fast should the first response happen?
5. Where are leads tracked now?
6. What follow-up gets missed most often?
7. What should happen when a lead does not respond?
8. What would a cleaner weekly view of leads need to show?

## Lead Record Creation Rule

Create a lead record under `agency-ops/leads/` only after a real prospect has been identified. Use the lead record template and include:

- prospect name,
- business type,
- observed intake signals,
- likely pain point,
- preliminary fit rating,
- current pipeline stage,
- recommended next action,
- source notes.

## Operating Notes

- Do not send outreach without explicit approval.
- Do not claim a prospect has a problem unless it is observed or confirmed.
- Keep the offer narrow: lead intake, qualification, follow-up prompts, and a simple owner-facing view.
- Prefer draft-first follow-up over auto-send behavior unless a client approves the message, timing, and rules.
- Update this workflow after real outreach reveals stronger niches, objections, or buying signals.
