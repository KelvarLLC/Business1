## Executive Summary

### Business Problem

After a prospect says yes, the handoff from sales to delivery often depends on scattered email notes, manual checklists, owner memory, and repeated client questions. This slows down project starts, creates missing-information loops, and makes the first client experience feel less organized than the sale.

### Proposed Automation Solution

Build a focused onboarding handoff workflow that captures the required client details, creates a clean internal handoff summary, starts the first delivery tasks, sends or drafts next-step communication, and flags missing information before work stalls.

### Expected Outcome

The client gets a more reliable post-sale start, fewer dropped setup details, clearer internal ownership, and a simple view of which new clients are ready to begin versus waiting on inputs.

## Scope

### Included Workflows

- Current onboarding process review and required-input mapping.
- Intake form, structured intake email, or intake checklist design.
- Internal handoff summary for new client setup.
- Task, checklist, tracker, or project-board creation for delivery start.
- Client next-step email draft or approved confirmation workflow.
- Missing-information reminders for owner, admin, or client.
- Simple onboarding status tracker with owner, blocker, and next action.
- Handoff documentation for operating and maintaining the workflow.

### Systems Involved

To be confirmed during discovery. Common systems may include:

- Email inbox.
- Form or questionnaire tool.
- Spreadsheet, CRM, task manager, or project board.
- Calendar or scheduling tool.
- Document storage or proposal/contract folder.
- Payment, contract, or approval status source, if relevant.

### Deliverables

- Current-state onboarding handoff map.
- Required intake fields and internal handoff structure.
- Configured onboarding tracker or task/checklist destination.
- Missing-information reminder or draft-message workflow.
- Client next-step message template.
- Test cases for ready-to-start, missing-info, and manual-review scenarios.
- Operating notes with owner responsibilities and maintenance guidance.

### Assumptions

- The initial workflow supports one standard onboarding path unless additional client types are explicitly scoped.
- The client can provide sample onboarding emails, intake questions, task lists, and tool access.
- Any contract, payment, compliance, or legally sensitive step remains human-reviewed unless separately approved.
- Automated client-facing messages require client approval before launch.

### Out Of Scope

- Full CRM or project-management migration.
- Contract drafting or legal review.
- Payment processing changes.
- Complex multi-department implementation.
- Custom software beyond the agreed automation workflow.
- Guaranteed delivery speed or revenue outcomes.

## Implementation Plan

1. Discovery and validation: confirm the post-sale handoff, required client inputs, internal owners, blockers, and tools.
2. Workflow design: define trigger, intake fields, handoff summary, task creation, reminder rules, and review points.
3. Build and integration: configure intake, tracker, task/checklist creation, reminders, and client next-step draft or confirmation.
4. Testing and rollout: test common onboarding scenarios, missing data, manual overrides, and client-facing copy.
5. Handoff and optimization: document operation, maintenance, and phase-two improvements.

## Dependencies

### Required Access

- Intake source, email account, or form tool.
- Tracker, CRM, task manager, project board, or spreadsheet destination.
- Notification or email tool used for reminders and client messages.
- Sample workspace or permission to create test onboarding records.

### Required Inputs

- Current onboarding checklist or rough step list.
- Required client information before delivery can start.
- Sample client next-step messages.
- Internal owner/admin roles.
- Escalation rules for missing information.
- Any review requirements for contract, payment, or compliance-related steps.

### External Blockers

- Missing tool access.
- Unclear owner responsibility after the sale.
- No agreed minimum onboarding checklist.
- Client approval needed for external messaging or timing rules.

## Risks And Mitigations

- Risk: The onboarding process varies too much by client type.
- Mitigation: Start with the most common client path and document special cases as phase-two scope.

- Risk: Sensitive contract or payment status is automated too aggressively.
- Mitigation: Keep those steps as visible human review checkpoints unless explicitly approved.

- Risk: Internal users ignore the tracker or tasks.
- Mitigation: Keep fields lean, surface only the next action, and align the workflow with existing owner/admin habits.

- Risk: Client-facing messages are sent before the copy is approved.
- Mitigation: Launch with draft-first messages or internal reminders until approval is recorded.

## Discovery Questions

1. What happens today immediately after a prospect says yes?
2. What information must be collected before delivery can start?
3. Which onboarding steps are repeated for most clients?
4. Where do client details, contracts, payment status, and setup notes currently live?
5. Who owns each step after the sale?
6. Which steps are most often delayed, forgotten, or unclear?
7. What should the client receive right after committing?
8. Which steps require human review before any automated action?
