## Proposal Decision Triggers

Use these triggers after a reply or discovery call.

### Move To Proposal Outline Ready

A lead can move to `proposal-outline-ready` when they confirm at least:

- One primary intake source.
- One current destination, such as email, CRM, form tool, spreadsheet, or shared inbox.
- One owner or owner group.
- One pain pattern, such as slow first response, unclear routing, stale follow-up, or no status view.
- Interest in seeing a scoped next step.

Still missing:

- Exact fields, access, timing, and implementation assumptions.

### Move To Proposal Draft Ready

A lead can move to `proposal-draft-ready` when they confirm:

- Primary intake source.
- Current tool/destination.
- First owner.
- Follow-up process.
- The first useful outcome.
- Safety expectation: internal reminders or drafts are acceptable before any auto-send.
- Decision-maker or stakeholder authority.
- Preferred package path from the scope menu.

### Keep Discovery Needed

Keep `discovery-needed` if:

- They are curious but vague.
- They ask for pricing without workflow details.
- They mention automation generally but not a lead-intake pain.
- They cannot name the intake source or owner.

### Move To Nurture Or Close

Move to `nurture-or-close` if:

- No lead-intake or follow-up pain exists.
- They only want broad AI consulting unrelated to the current offer.
- They want unmanaged auto-send as the first step.
- They ask for legal, insurance, compliance, or regulated advice outside scope.
- They are not a fit for service-business intake automation.

## Quick Reply-To-Proposal Triage

When a reply arrives, answer these before drafting anything:

1. Did they name a workflow pain?
2. Did they name the intake source?
3. Did they name the current tool or destination?
4. Did they name who owns the next action?
5. Did they describe urgency or business impact?
6. Did they ask for a call, a map, pricing, or implementation?
7. Did they accept a bounded first step?
8. Is auto-send off by default unless separately reviewed?

If five or more are clear, use the proposal decision scorecard.

If fewer than five are clear, use the discovery call script or reply response kit first.
